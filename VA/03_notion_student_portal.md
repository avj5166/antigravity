## ❓ FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

**Q: How long does the France visa take?**
A: Typically 15–21 working days after VFS submission. Can be up to 45 days in peak season (July–August).

**Q: Can I work in France as a student?**
A: Yes! Student visa allows you to work up to 964 hours/year (~20 hours/week). Minimum wage (SMIC) is ~€11.65/hour.

**Q: Do I need to know French to study in France?**
A: Not necessarily for English-taught Masters programs. However, learning basic French is highly recommended for daily life. Free resources: Duolingo, France Langue, Alliance Française.

**Q: What is VLS-TS and why must I validate it?**
A: VLS-TS (Visa de Long Séjour valant Titre de Séjour) is your student visa that also acts as a residence permit for Year 1. You MUST validate it on the ANEF website within 3 months of arrival. Failure means your visa is invalid.

**Q: Can I bring my family to France?**
A: After arriving, you can apply for "family reunification" but it has strict criteria. Spouse and children of students can apply for a separate visa. Discuss with counselor.

**Q: What if my visa is rejected?**
A: Don't panic. Work with your counselor to understand the rejection reason, address it, and re-apply. Many students succeed on their second application with the right documentation.
