### STAGE 06 — SOP / LOR Writing
**Who acts:** Consultancy + Student
**Typical Duration:** 10–21 days
**What happens:**

**Statement of Purpose (SOP):**
- Conduct 1-hour SOP interview with student via call/in-person
- Understand: Why France? Why this program? Why this university? Career goals?
- Draft SOP (750–1000 words for most programs)
- Tailored version for each university (mention specific professors, labs, programs)
- Student reviews → Consultancy revises → Final version
- SOP should cover: Academic background → Work/Research experience → Why this field → Why France → Why this university → Future goals

**Letters of Recommendation (LOR):**
- Identify 2–3 recommenders (professors, thesis supervisors, managers)
- Draft LOR template for recommender to use/modify
- Recommenders should know student's specific achievements
- LOR on official letterhead, signed, stamped

**Cover Letter (for visa):**
- Separate from SOP — this is for the French consulate
- Explains: Who you are, what you will study, where, why France, how you will fund it, your intention to return to India after studies
- Length: 1 page max, professional tone
- Must be factually consistent with all other documents

**Action for Consultancy:**
- Store final SOP versions in student's Google Drive folder
- Update Notion page when SOP finalized

**What student receives:**
- Final SOP (PDF)
- Final LOR templates
- Final Cover Letter
- WhatsApp: Script W-06

### STAGE 08 — Campus France Account Created
**Who acts:** Student (guided by consultancy)
**Typical Duration:** 1–3 days
**What happens:**
- India IS a Campus France mandatory country
- Student creates account on: **https://inde.campusfrance.org/**
- Steps:
  1. Go to Campus France India website
  2. Create a student account
  3. Fill in personal details, academic background
  4. Create the "dossier" (application file)
  5. Enter details of French institutions they're applying to
  6. Upload academic documents to Campus France profile
  7. Pay Campus France fee: **₹15,000–17,000** (non-refundable)

**Important:** Campus France reviews the student's academic profile and conducts an interview before issuing clearance. Without Campus France clearance, the French consulate will NOT process the visa.

**Action for Consultancy:**
- Sit with student (in person or video) to guide account creation
- Double-check all entries before submission — errors are hard to fix
- Note the Campus France reference number in Google Sheet

**What student receives:**
- WhatsApp: Script W-08
- Notion updated: Campus France Account ✅

### STAGE 10 — Campus France Interview Scheduled
**Who acts:** Campus France + Consultancy
**Typical Duration:** 2–4 weeks wait time
**What happens:**
- Campus France India schedules an interview
- Interview conducted: **In-person (New Delhi / Mumbai office) OR online (video call)**
- Campus France confirms date and time via email
- Interview is in ENGLISH (not French required)
- Duration: 20–40 minutes

**Interview Preparation (Consultancy does this with student):**
Common Questions Asked:
1. Why do you want to study in France specifically?
2. Why this particular program / university?
3. How will you fund your studies?
4. What are your career plans after graduation?
5. Do you plan to return to India after your studies?
6. How did you hear about this program?
7. Do you speak any French?
8. What do you know about France / French culture?
9. Walk me through your academic background
10. Why not study in India or another country?

**Key Principles:**
- Be honest — Campus France cross-checks everything
- Show strong ties to India (family, career opportunities)
- Be specific about the university and program — vague answers are red flags
- Enthusiasm for France (culture, education system) is positive

**Action for Consultancy:**
- Conduct mock interview session with student
- Note interview date in Sheet 4 (Deadlines Calendar)
- Send interview prep guide via WhatsApp

**What student receives:**
- WhatsApp: Script W-10 (Interview prep kit)
- 2-day before reminder: Script W-10b

### STAGE 15 — Visa Document Preparation
**Who acts:** Consultancy + Student
**Typical Duration:** 7–14 days
**What happens:**
This is the most critical and detail-sensitive stage. Reference Sheet 2 (Document Tracker) and verify EVERY document.

**Full France Student Visa (VLS-TS) Document List:**

**Identity Documents:**
- [ ] Original passport + copy (valid for 3 months beyond intended stay)
- [ ] 2 recent passport-size photos (35x45mm, white background, face 80% of frame)
- [ ] Copy of any previous visas (if applicable)

**Application Forms:**
- [ ] Completed Visa Application Form (CERFA 14571*05) — download from VFS website
- [ ] OFII form (filled but NOT signed — sign at VFS)

**Study Documents:**
- [ ] Campus France Attestation (original + copy)
- [ ] University Admission/Acceptance Letter (original + copy)
- [ ] University enrollment confirmation

**Academic Documents:**
- [ ] 10th marksheet + certificate (original + copy)
- [ ] 12th marksheet + certificate (original + copy)
- [ ] All semester marksheets (original + copy)
- [ ] Degree certificate (original + copy)
- [ ] Official transcripts from university
- [ ] Medium of instruction certificate

**Financial Documents (CRITICAL — most common rejection reason):**
- [ ] Personal/family bank statements: Last 6 months (showing sufficient funds)
- [ ] Minimum recommended: €615/month × duration of stay = total funds needed
- [ ] Example: 12-month Masters = €7,380 (~₹6.5–7L)
- [ ] Fixed deposits, investments (as additional proof)
- [ ] Scholarship letter (if applicable)
- [ ] Sponsor declaration (if parent/relative is sponsoring)
- [ ] Sponsor's bank statements
- [ ] Sponsor's income proof: salary slips, ITR (last 2 years)
- [ ] Relationship proof with sponsor (birth certificate, etc.)

**Accommodation in France:**
- [ ] Proof of accommodation for first months (university residence confirmation OR host family letter OR rental agreement)

**Insurance:**
- [ ] Health/travel insurance valid for France (minimum €30,000 coverage)

**Cover Letter:**
- [ ] Personal cover letter explaining purpose and plans

**Action for Consultancy:**
- Go through each item in Sheet 2 with student
- Check every document for validity (expiry dates, signatures, stamps)
- Arrange certified translations for any non-English/French documents
- Review financial documents — this is where most rejections happen
- Prepare a final document bundle checklist

**What student receives:**
- WhatsApp: Script W-15 (Visa prep begins!)
- Physical/digital checklist of every document with status

### STAGE 16 — VFS Appointment Booked
**Who acts:** Consultancy (or Student with guidance)
**Typical Duration:** Appointment availability varies (book ASAP)
**What happens:**
- VFS Global processes French visa applications in India
- VFS Centres in India for French Visa: New Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata, Chennai, Bangalore, Hyderabad
- Book appointment on: **https://visa.vfsglobal.com/ind/en/fra**
- Select: Long-stay visa (visa de long séjour)
- Select category: Student / Étudiant
- Pay VFS service fee online + Visa fee (€99 ≈ ₹9,000)
- VFS appointment slots can fill up FAST in peak season (June–August)
  → **Book appointment as early as possible — ideally 6–8 weeks before intended travel**

**Important Timing Notes:**
- Visa processing time: 15–21 business days (can be longer in peak season)
- Apply for visa NO EARLIER than 3 months before travel date
- Apply NO LATER than 6 weeks before travel date
- For September intake: Book VFS appointment by early July

**Action for Consultancy:**
- Book appointment immediately when documents are ready
- Save VFS appointment confirmation
- Note appointment date in Sheet 4 (Deadlines Calendar)
- Confirm appointment date with student

**What student receives:**
- WhatsApp: Script W-16 (Appointment confirmed!)
- VFS appointment details + what to bring on the day

### STAGE 17 — Visa Application Submitted
**Who acts:** Student (physically present at VFS)
**Typical Duration:** 1 day (VFS appointment)
**What happens:**
- Student goes to VFS centre in person (cannot send representative)
- Bring ALL original documents + copies in organized folder
- VFS staff will:
  - Verify all documents
  - Take biometric data (fingerprints + photo)
  - Collect the complete document file
  - Issue a receipt/tracking slip
- Documents are then sent to the French Consulate for decision

**Day-of Checklist for Student:**
- [ ] Arrive 15 minutes early
- [ ] Bring document folder (organized per checklist)
- [ ] Bring both original documents AND copies of each
- [ ] Bring passport (original)
- [ ] Bring VFS appointment confirmation
- [ ] Dress professionally (not required but advisable)
- [ ] Have all forms signed (EXCEPT OFII — sign at VFS when instructed)
- [ ] Have exact cash/card for any remaining VFS service fees

**Action for Consultancy:**
- Final document check 1 day before appointment (30-minute call with student)
- Give student a physical copy of the day-of checklist

**What student receives:**
- WhatsApp: Script W-17 (Night before: good luck + checklist reminder)
- WhatsApp: Script W-17b (After appointment: what happens next)

### STAGE 18 — Visa Under Processing
**Who acts:** French Consulate
**Typical Duration:** 15–21 working days (can be up to 45 days in peak season)
**What happens:**
- French Consulate reviews the complete application
- They may request additional documents (if so, VFS contacts student)
- Track application status online: **https://visa.vfsglobal.com** using tracking number
- Do NOT call VFS to "check status" — it doesn't help and is not reliable

**Common Reasons for Delays:**
- High season (Aug/Sep) — processing takes longer
- Additional document verification needed
- Financial proof queries
- Background check

**Common Reasons for Rejection:**
- Insufficient financial proof
- Inconsistent information across documents
- Doubts about intent to return to India
- Missing documents
- Campus France attestation issues (rare)
- Accommodation proof missing

**Action for Consultancy:**
- Check VFS tracking every 5–7 days
- Inform student of any updates
- Prepare student mentally for the wait

**What student receives:**
- WhatsApp: Script W-18 (Here's what to expect while waiting)
- Weekly update message until decision

### STAGE 19 — VISA APPROVED ✅
**Who acts:** VFS → Student
**Typical Duration:** 1 day for passport collection
**What happens:**
- VFS calls/SMS/emails student when passport is ready for collection
- Student collects passport from VFS centre
- Verify visa details:
  - Name spelling (matches passport)
  - Dates (entry before/on this date + duration of stay)
  - Number of entries (usually single entry for student visa)
  - Visa type (VLS-TS = Visa de Long Séjour valant Titre de Séjour)
- If any error on the visa — report immediately to VFS (do not travel)

**IMPORTANT — VLS-TS Validation in France:**
- The VLS-TS visa must be validated online within **3 months** of arrival in France
- Website: **https://administration-etrangers-en-france.interieur.gouv.fr/**
- This is MANDATORY — failure to validate means visa becomes invalid
- Brief student on this BEFORE they depart

**Action for Consultancy:**
- Congratulate student warmly (this is a celebration!)
- Schedule pre-departure briefing (Stage 21)
- Update Google Sheet: Visa Outcome = Approved ✅
- Update Notion: Visa Approved 🎉

**What student receives:**
- WhatsApp: Script W-19 (VISA APPROVED! 🎉🇫🇷)
- Pre-departure preparation guide begins

### STAGE 20 — VISA REJECTED ❌
**Who acts:** French Consulate
**Typical Duration:** Same as approval timeline
**What happens:**
- Rejection notice attached to passport when returned
- Rejection letter states the reason (must read carefully)
- Common reasons and solutions:

| Rejection Reason | Solution |
|---|---|
| Insufficient financial proof | Provide additional bank statements, FDs, sponsor documents |
| Incomplete documents | Re-apply with complete set |
| Doubt about intent to return | Stronger ties-to-India documents (property, family business) |
| Inconsistent information | Review all documents for consistency |
| Missing accommodation proof | Get confirmed student housing before re-applying |

**Re-application Options:**
1. Address the stated reason → Re-apply (new full application + fees)
2. Appeal (recours) — possible but rarely successful without new evidence
3. Apply for a different intake (next semester)

**Action for Consultancy:**
- Call student immediately — do not leave them alone with the rejection
- Analyze rejection letter carefully
- Discuss realistic options
- If re-applying: Begin process from Stage 15 with corrected documents

**What student receives:**
- WhatsApp: Script W-20 (empathetic, solution-focused message)
- In-person or video meeting to discuss next steps

### STAGE 21 — Pre-Departure Briefing
**Who acts:** Consultancy
**Typical Duration:** 1–2 hour session
**What happens:**
Conduct a thorough pre-departure briefing covering:

**Before You Leave India:**
- [ ] Book flights (check if student visa requires booking before visa — sometimes yes)
- [ ] Book temporary accommodation for first week (hostel, Airbnb, or student dorm)
- [ ] Get travel insurance (if not already done)
- [ ] Open an Indian forex card (Niyo, BookMyForex, HDFC Forex)
- [ ] Carry €500–1000 cash for first expenses
- [ ] Get international roaming activated (first few days until French SIM)
- [ ] Download offline maps of your university city
- [ ] Carry all original documents in hand luggage (NOT checked bags)
- [ ] Carry university acceptance letter + Campus France attestation in hand luggage

**In France — First Week:**
- [ ] Validate VLS-TS visa online within 3 months (do it in Week 1)
- [ ] Register at your university / international student office
- [ ] Get French SIM card (Free Mobile, Bouygues, Orange)
- [ ] Open a French bank account (Boursorama, Revolut, N26 work for students)
- [ ] Register with CAF (Caisse d'Allocations Familiales) for housing allowance (APL)
- [ ] Apply for Carte Vitale (French health insurance registration)
- [ ] Explore your city — you've worked hard for this!

**Action for Consultancy:**
- Share pre-departure resource pack (Document 05)
- Ask student to stay in touch after arriving — request a "landed safely" message
- Request a Google Review after they've settled (this builds your reputation)

**What student receives:**
- WhatsApp: Script W-21 (Pre-departure kit)
- Pre-departure checklist PDF
